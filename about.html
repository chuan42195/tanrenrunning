鍛錬

Why Tanren

The word is Japanese. 鍛錬.

It describes the ancient process of forging a blade — heating the steel, folding it, hammering it flat, repeating hundreds of times until what was raw and impure becomes something refined, resilient and extraordinary.

I chose it because it is the most accurate description I know of what actually happens when an endurance athlete improves.

Not the race. Not the long run that earns a Strava segment. Not the interval session that leaves you horizontal on the track.

The accumulation. The layers. The Monday easy run that felt pointless. The Thursday tempo that felt ordinary. The Sunday long run you finished tired and doubting. Week after week, month after month — each session a fold in the steel, each block a layer you cannot yet see.

And then, one day, on a start line, you are different. You do not know exactly when it happened. But the blade is sharper than it was.

That is the process I coach.

The Background

I am a World Athletics Level 2 certified coach in middle and long distance running.

My coaching sits at the intersection of exercise science and practical experience. But that experience wasn't inherited; it was built from the ground up. I started running late in life with little to no guidance, finishing my first marathon in 6:30. I know what it's like to feel lost in the process, struggling through the "trial and error" phase that breaks so many runners.

It was only by moving away from guesswork and applying the principles of structured load management and aerobic development that I was able to achieve a 2:42 marathon PB.

These numbers matter less than what chasing them taught me — about training structure, how the body adapts under sustained consistent stress, and how to translate that knowledge into practical coaching for serious recreational athletes. Based in Singapore, I have spent years training in heat and humidity that would break most generic programs. This environment demands a level of precision that translates directly to performance anywhere in the world — whether you are racing locally or lining up at Gold Coast, Tokyo, or Berlin.

I believe in educating athletes to understand their training, not just follow it. My goal is to be the guide I didn't have at the start — ensuring your journey to a breakthrough is shorter, more sustainable, and far more achievable than doing it alone.

Who I Coach

I work with distance runners — half marathon, marathon and ultra — and multisport athletes for whom running is the discipline that needs the most structured development. Hyrox competitors, triathletes, duathletes, OCR athletes.

The methodology is the same regardless of the sport. The aerobic base is the foundation. Intelligent load management is the strategy. Consistency is the differentiator. Only the specific architecture of each programme changes to match the demands of the target event.

I do not sell eight-week transformation programmes. I work with athletes who are already serious about their sport and want the structure and expertise to take their performance further.

I also do not hand you a generic group programme built for the average athlete. If you have been following a run club plan and wondering why the progress stopped — or why the injuries started — that is exactly the problem structured individual coaching exists to solve. Group programmes produce early gains because any structure beats none. But they are not built around where you specifically are, and they will not take you where you specifically want to go.

If that is you, I would like to hear from you.

How I Coach

Aerobic Base Above Everything

The majority of your training should feel easy. Not moderately hard. Easy. Conversational. This is not a comfortable suggestion for athletes who equate effort with progress. It is however what the evidence and the results consistently support.

Load You Can Actually Absorb

Training stress is only useful if your body can absorb and adapt to it. Too much too soon is not ambition — it is the most common cause of the injury cycle that derails most recreational athletes every twelve to eighteen months.

Education, Not Dependency

My goal is for every athlete I coach to understand their training deeply enough that they could eventually design their own. An athlete who understands the principles trains with more intention and less anxiety than one who simply follows instructions.
